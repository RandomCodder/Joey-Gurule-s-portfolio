Homeless Helper

A mobile app I designed in Figma.com in App Academy in my sophomore year of high school.

Idea

The first big project I did in my high school. This project is a mobile app that I designed in Figma.com in which the goal is to find homeless people, locate and give them food. There are screens that featured a map to locate, person's bio, statistics, and a separate map to locate restaurants. The restaurants would have a care package of food given at a lower price for the user to order to give it to the homeless.

I wanted to create this because I thought it was a cool idea. A lot of people in my class made games which was fine, but I wanted to create something that was related to a real life issue, in which it was people in poverty. This idea was well respected from my teacher and others because the idea was so wholesome.


Developing

The learning material was interesting. We went over everything we needed to know to make an app in Figma.com. This was my first time using the website so there was a bit to cover. We went over how to make frames, how to import icons to our prototpye, how CSS worked, how to add nodes to navigate from screen to screen, and how to run tests on the prototype.

At first, I was a bit overwhelmed with how much info I needed to take in, but then I realized that when you learn it, its mostly copy paste info, just different text. We also learned how to import icons and having those icons fitted with button functionality and have them change when you hover the mouse over it. This was fun to do and in my opinion, designing the background on the screens was my favorite thing to do.

From Figma to Code.org

There was a time near the end of the school where the whole class experience began to experience an issue related to Figma.com. The website went down and began to experience issues with loading. None of our files would load and when we tried to launch our prototype, it would just load forever. At first, all we did was wait until the site was back up, or began to work again but sadly, nothing. We waited for I think a week or 2 and still, the site wasn't loading. Because of this disadvantage, our teacher decided for us to remake our prototype app using a different website known as Code.org. Prior to this, we used this website in the past when making our game projects, so we all knew how everything was set up to use. The only thing we needed to learn was how to use the app template and how making apps worked.

This transition for me wasn't that bad. The learning material was straight forward, I understood what to do, and developing the app on Code.org was ok. I actually was able to recreate the app in just 2 days and since I was caught up, I began to work on a google slides presentation related to my prototype. Code.org was really easy to work with to bulid my prototype since the code for me was more straight forward and better to understand. Visually, the app looks slightly different from Figma.com, but works very similarly from the same purpose.
